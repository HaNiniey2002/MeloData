About

MeloData is an interactive music analytics platform that provides insights into how song attributes influence human brain responses. It explores the relationship between musical elements such as tempo, energy, valence, and energy; and their effects on mood, focus, emotional states, and physical reactions.

This platform combines informative content with an interactive analytics dashboard, allowing users to visualize how song attributes evolve over time and how these attributes shape listener appeal and emotional expression. Through dynamic data visualizations, MeloData helps users identify music trends, understand emotional patterns in songs, and explore how different musical features impact listener experiences. MeloData is designed for users who are interested in music analysis, cognitive responses to sound, and data-driven exploration of music trends.

MeloData is designed for users who have an interest in music, data analysis, and emotional responses to sound. This platform is suitable for students, researchers, and general music listeners who want to explore how song attributes such as tempo, energy, and valence influence mood, focus, and emotional experience. MeloData is also useful for users who are curious about music trends over time and prefer learning through interactive and visual dashboards. By combining data-driven insights with simple visualizations, MeloData allows users with no technical background to easily understand and explore music patterns.

Dashboard

The dashboard contains four sections:

Home

To introduce the MeloData dashboard, its purpose, and key features for exploring music attribute trends over time.

Overview

To present a summary of key music attributes and their definitions, helping users understand the metrics used in the dashboard.

Analysis

To provide detailed visualizations and insights into specific music attributes, enabling users to explore correlations and distributions within the data.

Prediction

To forecast future trends in music attributes using historical data, helping users anticipate how music may evolve in the coming years.
